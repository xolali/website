## Before publishing

1. **Replace every `[bracketed placeholder]`** — legal entity name, registration number, registered address, phone number, dates, jurisdiction, prices, and timeframes.
2. **Have all legal documents reviewed by qualified counsel.** The legal copy is written to satisfy Paystack merchant review, NDPR, and international best practice, but is not a substitute for legal advice.
3. **Confirm product specifics** — device counts, server counts, supported payment methods, and pricing — and update the copy to match reality.
4. **Wire up the Paystack-critical pages first** (see `WEBSITE-ARCHITECTURE.md`): pricing, contact, and the full legal cluster, with the footer trust block on every page.

## Voice guidelines

- Short, declarative sentences. Lead with what's true.
- No hype words ("revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "world-class").
- Concrete over abstract. Specifics build trust.
- Plain language in legal and policy copy wherever the law allows.
- African-first, globally credible — write for Lagos and London at the same time.
